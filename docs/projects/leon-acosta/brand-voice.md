# Leon Acosta - Personal Brand Voice Guide

## Who is Leon?
- Founder of Dandelion Labs (AI development agency)
- Based in Switzerland
- Building AI products and helping startups ship MVPs fast
- Early adopter of AI agents (has Robert, an AI companion)

## Brand Positioning
**Tagline idea:** "Building AI, one MVP at a time"

**What makes Leon unique:**
- Actually ships AI products (not just talks about AI)
- Runs an agency that delivers in 6 weeks, not 6 months
- Experiments with cutting-edge AI (agents, automation)
- Swiss precision meets startup speed

## Voice & Tone

### DO:
- Be direct and practical (no fluff)
- Share real experiences and lessons learned
- Be opinionated about AI development
- Use "I" - this is personal, not corporate
- Include specific numbers and examples when possible
- Show personality and humor occasionally
- Ask questions to spark conversation

### DON'T:
- Sound like a corporate press release
- Use buzzword soup without substance
- Brag without providing value
- Post just to post (quality over frequency)
- Be preachy or condescending

## Content Pillars

### 1. Building in Public (40%)
- Agency wins and learnings
- Project insights (without breaking client confidentiality)
- Behind-the-scenes of running an AI agency
- Mistakes and what they taught

### 2. AI Practitioner Insights (35%)
- What actually works vs. hype
- Technical decisions explained simply
- Tool and framework opinions
- Predictions based on hands-on experience

### 3. Founder Journey (25%)
- Startup life observations
- Switzerland tech scene
- Networking and community
- Personal growth and reflections

## Posting Guidelines

**Frequency:** 3-4x per week
**Best times (CET):** Tue-Thu, 8-9 AM or 5-6 PM
**Format mix:**
- Text posts (70%)
- Carousels/documents (20%)
- Reposts with commentary (10%)

**Engagement strategy:**
- Reply to every comment on your posts
- Comment thoughtfully on 5-10 posts daily from your network
- Connect with people who engage meaningfully

## Example Posts

### Good example:
```
We just shipped an AI MVP in 4 weeks.

The client came to us after 3 months of failed attempts with their in-house team.

What changed?
- Smaller scope (MVP, not "the vision")
- Pre-built components instead of from scratch
```
